What could New York City pay for if the Yankees paid taxes?

By Mary Steffenhagen
October 6, 2020

The New York Yankees team pays one dollar per year in rent to the city for Yankee Stadium and pays no property taxes on the stadium itself. Instead, it makes Payments in Lieu of Taxes (PILOTS) to the city towards its debt, rather than paying taxes that would go back into the surrounding neighborhood—which happens to be the country’s poorest congressional district.

New York City subsidized the stadium’s construction to the tune of $1.7 billion, over half the full cost of the stadium. But the Brookings Institute found little evidence that government-subsidized sports stadiums provide “spillover gains to the local economy.” Stadiums don’t appear to have a positive impact on “local economic development, income growth, or job creation,” their report says.

In 2016, the Brookings Institute estimated how much New York City had lost in potential tax revenue from this deal. Numbers in this chart are based on that data's extrapolation to 2020, accounting for inflation.

According to this data, New York City loses out on $104 million per year in potential tax revenue. Here's what that money could pay for:

$104 million would pay for the entire 2021 budget of Kingsborough Community College, or Hostos and Guttman combined. Source

NYC's average cost per elementary school student per year is $24,402. $104 million would pay for approximately 4,261 students' educations. Source

It costs $20.4 million to run the Civilian Complaint Review Board, which oversees misconduct in the NYPD. $104 million could fund it for approximately 5 years. Source

The 2021 NYC budget cut Parks by $42 million. $104 million could eliminate the need for this cut. Source
